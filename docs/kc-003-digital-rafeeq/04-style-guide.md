---

## Purpose of This Document

This style guide ensures Digital Rafeeq uses a **consistent, respectful, Urdu-first conversational style** across all interaction surfaces — aligned with campaign culture, [10-conversation-principles.md](./10-conversation-principles.md), and Karkun Connect's existing presentation standards where applicable.

---

## Design Posture

Digital Rafeeq's language should feel like a **trusted companion** supporting Rukn work — not a corporate bot, novelty assistant, or impersonal help desk.

Style rules apply equally to **written conversation** (chat, WhatsApp) and **spoken conversation** (voice interaction), adapted per surface without changing underlying principles.

---

## Document Scope

This document will eventually cover:

- Conversation style and tone principles
- Urdu-first language policy (script, register, transliteration)
- Address forms and honorifics
- Vocabulary standards (domain terms vs. plain Urdu)
- Message length and structure
- Emoji, punctuation, and formatting conventions
- Bilingual fallback rules (Urdu / English)
- Inclusive and culturally appropriate phrasing
- Examples and counter-examples
